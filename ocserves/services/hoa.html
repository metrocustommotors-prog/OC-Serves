HOA service done right the first time

Serving a homeowners association is rarely as simple as knocking on a door. An HOA may be served through its designated agent for service of process, an officer or director of the board, or in some cases its management company — and the correct path depends on how the association is organized.

OC Serves works HOA matters across Orange County constantly. We know the management companies, we know how to identify the current board, and we know how to verify the association's agent of record before an attempt is made. That groundwork is what keeps an HOA serve from being challenged.

Whether you represent an owner against an association, an association against an owner, or a vendor in a contract dispute, we get your documents to the right party with a clean affidavit.

Why HOA Serves Go Wrong

The mistakes we help you avoid.

01

Wrong Entity

Serving the management company when the association itself is the proper party — or vice versa. We confirm which entity your matter actually requires.

02

Outdated Board

HOA boards turn over. We work to identify current officers and directors rather than relying on stale records.

03

Unverified Agent

Many associations have a designated agent for service. We verify the current agent of record before attempting service.
